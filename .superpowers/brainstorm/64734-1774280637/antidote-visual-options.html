A: Cloud Buttons

Use the same cloud-shaped buttons as your category filter. Consistent with the site's identity. Just the word — no emojis, no one-liners. Clean, playful, distinctive. When active, the cloud fills with deeper color.

Pros: Brand-consistent, proven design element, minimal
Cons: Less explanatory — first-timers might not know what "Prijaznost" filters

B: Minimal Cards + Subtext

Clean rectangular cards with the antidote word + a quiet one-liner below. No emojis, no heavy styling. The subtext explains WHY you'd click — "Ko dvomiš v ljudi" tells you this is for moments of cynicism.

Pros: Self-explanatory, elegant, guides first-timers
Cons: More rectangular/grid-like, less playful than clouds

C: Clouds + Whisper Subtext

Cloud-shaped buttons (your signature) with a tiny whisper of context below each one. The cloud is the clickable element, the subtext is just a quiet hint. Best of both: playful + explanatory. Subtext fades on hover as the cloud lifts.

Pros: Brand-consistent AND self-explanatory, most polished
Cons: Slightly more visual weight than pure clouds

💡 My take

C (Clouds + Whisper) — It's your signature design + just enough context for a first-timer to understand what each button does. The subtext is so quiet it doesn't compete with the cloud word, but it answers the question "why would I click this?" Pure clouds (A) look great but leave new visitors guessing. Cards (B) are clear but lose the magic of the cloud shapes that make your site distinctive.
