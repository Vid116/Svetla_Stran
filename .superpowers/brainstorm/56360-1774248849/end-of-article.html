End-of-Article: The Conversion Moment

Someone just finished reading. Current flow: share bar → related articles → newsletter → comments. How do we redesign this into a journey that keeps them?

Current end-of-article stack (top to bottom)

📄 Article body ends
── mid-article CTA (newsletter, if 4+ paragraphs) ──
📖 Long-form deep read (expandable)
📚 Sources list
🔍 Research & verification details
── THE GAP: reader is done, what now? ──
🔗 Share bar ("Vam je bila zgodba všeč?")
🏷️ Category navigation button
📰 Related articles (3 cards, same category)
✉️ Newsletter signup
💬 Comments

Proposed new flow — does this sequence feel right?

A

Emotion-First Flow

📄 Article body ends
📖 Long-form / Sources / Research (as-is)
── NEW: Emotional tag ──
"Ta zgodba je zdravilo za cinizem"
Small emotion pills: ☀️ UPANJE · 💛 TOPLINA
── NEW: Emotional next-read ──
"Če te je ta zgodba ogrela..."
2-3 cards matched by EMOTION, not category
Each card shows its emotion pill too
🔗 Share bar (kept, works well)
── REFRAMED: Daily Dose signup ──
"Dnevna doza dobrega"
Not "newsletter" — "one story a day as
an antidote for doom-scrolling"
💬 Comments (kept at bottom)

Key change: emotions BEFORE share/newsletter — the emotional moment creates connection, THEN you ask for the share/signup. Category button removed (replaced by emotion-matched cards).

B

Share-First Flow

📄 Article body ends
📖 Long-form / Sources / Research (as-is)
🔗 Share bar (immediately — capitalize on emotion)
── NEW: Emotional bridge ──
"Zdravilo za cinizem · ☀️ UPANJE · 💛 TOPLINA"
Compact, one-line, not a section
── COMBINED: Next reads + signup ──
"Kaj bereš naprej?"
2-3 emotion-matched article cards
Small "Dnevna doza" signup inline below cards
(not a big section, just email input + "Pošlji")
💬 Comments

Key change: Share comes FIRST while emotion is hottest. Emotion tag is compact context, not a section. Next reads and signup are combined into one "what's next" block. Leaner overall.

Key decisions embedded here

  • "Dnevna doza" vs "Newsletter": Both flows reframe the signup. "Get one positive story a day" is more compelling than "subscribe to our newsletter." Same Brevo backend, different framing.
  • Emotion-matched vs category-matched related articles: Currently related articles use same category. Both flows switch to matching by emotion/antidote data. A JUNAKI story about rescue workers and a ZIVALI story about a rescued dog share TOPLINA — they should be connected.
  • Comments stay at the very bottom: They're important for community but shouldn't interrupt the conversion flow.
