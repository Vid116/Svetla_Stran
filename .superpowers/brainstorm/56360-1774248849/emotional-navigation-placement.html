Where does emotional navigation live?

The "antidote" identity needs to be visible but not forced. Where should readers encounter it?

Context

You have 6 antidote types: jeza → prijaznost, skrb → upanje, cinizem → dokaz dobrote, osamljenost → povezanost, obup → odpornost, strah → pogum. And 5 emotions: PONOS, TOPLINA, OLAJŠANJE, ČUDENJE, UPANJE.

The question is where these surface — this affects whether emotional browsing feels natural or bolted-on.

A

Woven Into Existing UI

Emotions appear naturally within what already exists:

  • Article cards get a small emotion tag (e.g., a subtle "☀️ upanje" pill next to the category pill)
  • Article page shows "Zdravilo za cinizem" as a quiet line near the category/date header
  • Related articles section becomes "If this warmed you, try these" with emotion-matched stories
  • Category clouds get an optional "po občutku" toggle that switches from categories to emotions

Subtle. Discoverable. Doesn't break existing UX.

B

Dedicated "Kaj potrebuješ?" Section on Homepage

A prominent section on the homepage — between hero and article grid — that asks:

"Kaj potrebuješ danes?" (What do you need today?)

  • Six beautiful cards/buttons: Pogum, Upanje, Toplina, Ponos, Čudenje, Olajšanje
  • Clicking one filters the grid to stories with that emotion/antidote
  • Each card has a one-liner: "Pogum — za trenutke ko se svet zdi nevaren"
  • Article pages still show the emotion tag, but the homepage section is the main entry point

Bold. Makes the philosophy impossible to miss. Statement piece.

C

Dual Mode: Categories ↔ Emotions Toggle

The existing category cloud gets a mode switch. One button toggles between:

  • 📂 Po temi (by topic) — current category clouds: JUNAKI, SPORT, NARAVA...
  • 💛 Po občutku (by feeling) — emotion clouds: Upanje, Toplina, Pogum, Ponos, Čudenje, Olajšanje

Same cloud button design, same grid behavior, just different filter dimension. The toggle sits above the clouds.

On article pages: both category AND emotion shown in header. Related articles can mix both dimensions.

Elegant. Reuses existing design language. Gives readers agency.

💡 My take

C (dual mode) is the most elegant — it reuses your beautiful cloud buttons, doesn't add UI weight, and the toggle itself communicates "this site thinks about news differently." But B makes the biggest first impression for new visitors who might not notice a small toggle. What if we combine: B on first visit (the "kaj potrebuješ" moment), then C as the persistent tool for returning readers?
