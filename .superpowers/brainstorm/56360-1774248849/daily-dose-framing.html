"Dnevna doza" — How aggressive should the signup be?

The newsletter is being reframed from "subscribe" to "daily dose of good." But it currently appears in 4 places. How do we handle the new framing across the site?

Current newsletter touchpoints

  1. Homepage hero section — big signup block after article grid
  2. Mid-article CTA — compact inline after 3rd paragraph
  3. End-of-article — inline card below related articles
  4. Sticky mobile bar — appears after 35% scroll, dismissible

How should the "Dnevna doza" framing affect these?

A

Gentle — Two Touchpoints Only

  • Homepage: "Dnevna doza" section replaces current newsletter hero — warm, inviting, one-liner + email input
  • End-of-article: Compact "Dnevna doza" after emotion-matched articles (as designed in Flow A)
  • Remove: mid-article CTA and sticky mobile bar

Less pushy. Trusts the content to do the work. Risk: fewer conversions.

B

Strategic — Three Touchpoints, Context-Aware

  • Homepage: "Dnevna doza" section — prominent but warm
  • Mid-article: Reframed as emotion-aware: "Všeč ti je ta zgodba? Vsak dan ena taka v tvoj inbox." — shows only on long articles (5+ paragraphs)
  • End-of-article: Compact "Dnevna doza" in the new flow (after emotion cards, before comments)
  • Remove: sticky mobile bar (feels desperate with the new framing)

Balanced. Multiple opportunities without feeling spammy. Each appearance has different context.

C

Bold — Keep All Four, Fully Reframed

  • Homepage: "Dnevna doza" hero section
  • Mid-article: "Zdravilo za doom-scrolling — ena zgodba na dan"
  • End-of-article: Full "Dnevna doza" in new flow
  • Sticky mobile: Reframed: "☀️ Dnevna doza dobrega" with email input — appears once, remembers dismissal

Maximum conversion surface. Risk: could feel pushy despite the warm framing.

💡 My take

B (Strategic) — Three touchpoints, each with different context. The sticky bar always felt like the weakest one anyway (desperate energy). But the mid-article one is actually powerful when reframed: if someone is mid-read and enjoying the story, "ena taka vsak dan" is a genuine value proposition, not an interruption. The key is that all three feel like the SAME voice, just at different moments.
