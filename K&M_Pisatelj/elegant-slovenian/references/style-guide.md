## 1. Sentence Architecture

### Rhythm Variation

The single most powerful tool. Flat prose has uniform sentence length. Elegant prose
alternates:

**Flat:**
> Prišel sem na trg. Na trgu je bilo veliko ljudi. Vsi so se pogovarjali.
> Zrak je bil topel. Sonce je svetilo. Bil sem vesel.

**Rhythmically varied:**
> Na trgu — množica, pogovori, topel zrak. Sonce je razlivalo zlato svetlobo po
> starih fasadah. In nekje znotraj, tiho: veselje.

Techniques:
- Use a fragment for emphasis (not a full sentence — just a phrase, an image)
- Follow three short statements with one long, flowing sentence
- End a paragraph with something brief and resonant

### Inverzija (Inverted Word Order)

Kette's signature. In standard Slovenian: "Trudna noč molči." Kette writes: "Noč
trudna molči." The noun comes first, the adjective follows — this foregrounds the
image before qualifying it.

Use in prose:
- "Mesto staro je dihalo v večernem mraku" (not "Staro mesto je dihalo...")
- "Pot dolga se je vila med griči" (not "Dolga pot se je vila...")

**Rule: maximum 1–2 inversions per paragraph.** More than that and the prose becomes
affectedly archaic.

### Enjambement in Prose

Murn's verse frequently runs thoughts across line breaks. In prose, you can achieve a
similar effect by letting a sentence's meaning spill into the next:

> Vedel sem, da se moram odločiti. Ne danes,
> ne jutri, a kmalu. Preden me odločitev sama najde.

The dash, the continuation, the brief final clause — this creates forward momentum.

## 2. Word Choice & Vocabulary

### Vivid Verbs Over Copulas

The biggest upgrade in flat Slovenian. Replace "biti" constructions:

| Flat | Elegant |
|------|---------|
| Bilo je tiho | Tišina je legla na... |
| Nebo je bilo rdeče | Nebo je žarelo / rdečica je oblila nebo |
| Bil sem žalosten | Žalost me je objela / prevzela |
| Voda je bila mrzla | Voda je rezala v prste |
| Cesta je bila dolga | Cesta se je vlekla / vijugala |
| Dan je bil lep | Dan se je odprl / razcvetel |

### Specific Over Generic Adjectives

Avoid the "big five" empty adjectives (lep, velik, dober, slab, zanimiv) unless you
have a specific reason. Replace with something the reader can *feel*:

| Generic | Specific |
|---------|----------|
| lepa pokrajina | zelena, valovita pokrajina |
| velik prostor | prostran / brezkončen prostor |
| dober občutek | topel / mehak občutek |
| zanimiva zgodba | presenetljiva / skrivnostna zgodba |

### Pomanjševalnice (Diminutives)

Murn used them for emotional warmth: "vodice, rosice." In prose, diminutives add
tenderness or intimacy:

- "hišica" instead of "hiša" when describing something dear or small
- "potok" → "potokec" for a tiny stream
- "glas" → "glasek" for a faint voice

Use sparingly — one per passage at most, and only when the emotional register calls
for it.

### Slovenian Folk Texture

Both poets drew from folk song traditions, but they used folk flavor *selectively* —
a word here, a rhythm there — not wholesale archaism. Words that carry gentle folk
resonance without sounding old-fashioned:

- "rosni" as an adjective (dewy, fresh) — still used and understood
- "tih" and "molk" for silence — these are not archaic, just less common than "tišina"
- Natural vocabulary: "jasa", "deblo", "šum", "rosa", "zora", "mrak"
- "dekle" — use only in clearly literary/storytelling register, never in emails or blog posts

**Do NOT use** these supposedly folk/poetic words that are actually just archaic:
- "kedaj" — nobody says this anymore, use "kdaj"
- "zlat" instead of "zlaten" — archaic shortening, avoid
- "dasi" — dead word, use "čeprav"
- "nikar" — mostly archaic outside of very specific dialects
- "le" as a particle everywhere — modern Slovenian uses it sparingly

### Words to Avoid in Elegant Prose

These weaken Slovenian prose:

- "nekako" (vagueness filler)
- "v bistvu" / "pravzaprav" (hedging — either commit or rephrase)
- "super" / "kul" (slang — unless the register is deliberately casual)
- "ta" used as an empty article (a common calque from German/English)
- Excessive "zelo" — find a stronger word instead of modifying a weak one
- "potem... potem... potem..." — the temporal connector parade

## 3. Sound Patterns (Evfonija)

### Blagoglasje (Euphony)

Murn was praised for his "ubran zvok besed" — the harmonious sound of words. In prose:

- **Prefer liquid sounds** (l, r, m, n) in passages meant to feel soft or flowing
- **Use sibilants** (s, š, z, ž) for hissing, rushing, or atmospheric effects
  (as Murn did: "šumniki in sičniki ponazarjajo šumenje in pršenje vode")
- **Avoid consonant pileups** — if three hard consonants crash together, restructure
- Read the prose aloud (mentally). If it stumbles, rewrite.

### Repetition for Effect

Controlled repetition is powerful:

> Sneg je padal. Padal je na streho, padal na cesto, padal v temo.

The repeated "padal" builds a hypnotic, accumulative effect. This is different from
*accidental* repetition (repeating a word because you can't think of another), which
should be eliminated.

### Internal Rhyme

Occasional internal rhyme in prose creates musicality without being obvious:

> "Topel mrak se je razlil po poljih in doljih."

"poljih in doljih" rhymes subtly — this adds a folk-song quality without being forced.

## 4. Figurative Language

### Poosebitev (Personification)

Kette's "Na trgu" is built on personification: night is tired and flees, the moon
dreams, the wind plays with water. In prose:

- Give natural phenomena human qualities: "jutranja megla je tiho dihala",
  "sonce se je leno dvignilo", "veter je šepetal"
- Give abstract concepts physical presence: "tišina je legla na mesto",
  "spomin ga je objel"
- Keep it natural — the personification should feel like something a speaker of
  Slovenian might naturally say, not like a deliberate literary device

### Metonimija (Metonymy)

Replace a thing with something associated with it:

- "streha" for "dom" (roof for home)
- "zemlja" for "domovina" (earth/soil for homeland)
- "roke" for "delo" (hands for work)

### Čutne podobe (Sensory Images) — The Impressionist Core

This is the single most important technique from Murn. Layer sensory impressions:

**Before:** "Jesen je bila lepa."

**After:** "Listje je gorelo v rjavih in zlatih odtenkih, v zraku je dišalo po
vlažni zemlji in zadnjih jabolkih, nekje daleč pa se je slišal odmev cerkvenih
zvonov."

The "after" gives us sight (colors), smell (damp earth, apples), and sound (bells).
Three senses in one sentence — that's Murn's method.

## 5. Slovenian-Specific Pitfalls

### Germanisms in Syntax

Slovenian prose often unconsciously copies German word order. Watch for:

- Verb at the end of subordinate clauses (German habit): restructure to more
  natural Slovenian flow
- Overly long noun phrases before the verb (German nominal style)
- "Kar se tiče..." — a calque from "Was ... betrifft" — usually better as
  "Glede..." or restructured entirely

### Anglicisms in Style

Modern Slovenian increasingly mimics English patterns:

- Passive overuse: "Bil je vprašan" → "Vprašali so ga"
- Progressive tense calque: "Sem delal" when simple past suffices
- Excessive use of "jaz" (Slovenian is a pro-drop language — the verb endings
  carry the person; you don't need to state "jaz" unless for emphasis)

### The Dual Number

Slovenian has a grammatical dual. Don't lose it in transformation — it's a
uniquely Slovenian feature that adds precision and beauty:

- "Oba sva stala in gledala" (not "Mi smo stali")
- The dual can feel intimate: two people, two hands, two eyes — the grammar
  itself suggests closeness

## 6. Register Calibration Examples

### Email (Light Touch)

**Input:**
> Pozdravljeni. Pišem vam zato ker bi rad zvedel več o vašem programu.
> Zanima me kdaj se začne in kolko stane. Lp

**Output:**
> Pozdravljeni,
> z zanimanjem sem zasledil vaš program in bi rad izvedel kaj več —
> zlasti o začetku in ceni. Bom hvaležen za odgovor.
> Lep pozdrav

### Blog Post (Medium Touch)

**Input:**
> Ljubljana je lepo mesto. Ima veliko kavarnic. Ljudje so prijazni.
> Poleti je zelo toplo. Če greš v Ljubljano moraš iti na Prešernov trg.

**Output:**
> Ljubljana se ti ne vsili. Najprej zavohaš kavo iz odprtih vrat kakšne
> kavarne, potem te kdo prijazno pogleda na ulici, in nekako si že notri.
> Poleti je vroče, fasade se segrejejo, Ljubljanica teče počasi pod pilovi.
> Na Prešernov trg moraš, to ja. Ampak ne zato, ker ti je kdo rekel,
> temveč ker se tam vse začne in konča.

### Creative Description (Full Touch)

**Input:**
> Zjutraj je bila megla. Polja so bila mokra. Videl sem kmeta ki je šel
> na njivo. Imel je motiko. Bilo je hladno.

**Output:**
> Megla se je držala nizko čez polja, zemlja je bila mokra in težka.
> V sivini se je pokazal kmet, motiko čez ramo, in molče stopal proti
> njivi. Prsti so mu bile gotovo ledene, a korak je bil trden, vajen,
> kot vsako jutro.

## 7. AI Writing Patterns to Avoid

This section is a checklist. Before finalizing any transformation, scan the output
against these patterns. If you find more than two, rewrite.

### The AI Slovenian Starter Pack

These are the words and structures AI reaches for first. They are not *wrong* — they
are just overused to the point where they signal "this was generated":

| AI default | Alternatives |
|------------|-------------|
| zlata svetloba | bleda / tanka / ostra / mlačna svetloba |
| tišina (in every paragraph) | molk, mir, ali opis konkretnih tihih zvokov |
| X je dihal/a (personif.) | X je ležal/a, se razlival/a, čepal/a, molčal/a |
| objeti (za abstraktne pojme) | prevzeti, prešiniti, obliti, prestreči |
| obris | senca, podoba, lik, ali neposreden opis |
| razprostirati se | razlivati se, segati, ležati |
| prepleten/a | povezan/a, prežet/a, poln/a |
| za hip / za trenutek | pogosto nepotrebno, izpusti |

### Structural AI Patterns

- **Triplet addiction**: "X, Y in Z" more than twice per text = AI signal
- **Em dash parade**: more than 1 per paragraph = AI signal
- **Every paragraph ends profoundly**: vary endings, let some just stop
- **Relentless beauty**: not every sentence needs to shimmer; include plain ones
- **Colon-then-list**: "Videl je tri reči: X, Y in Z" — just list them naturally
- **Perfect parallel structure**: real prose has asymmetry

### Quick Sanity Check

Read your output and honestly answer:
1. Would this pass as written by Goran Vojnović, Dušan Šarotar, or Polona Glavan?
2. Or does it read like a "write me something poetic" ChatGPT output?

If the answer is closer to #2, strip out the decorative elements until it feels
like a real person wrote it. Elegance in prose is *restraint*, not decoration.
