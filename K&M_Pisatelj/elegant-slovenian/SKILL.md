# Elegant Slovenian — In the Spirit of Kette & Murn

## What This Skill Does

You receive Slovenian text that is flat, clumsy, repetitive, or stylistically dull. Your job
is to rewrite it as **elegant Slovenian prose** — not poetry, but prose that carries the
literary DNA of the Slovenian moderna, specifically the sensibilities of **Dragotin Kette**
and **Josip Murn Aleksandrov**.

The goal is not to imitate their poems. The goal is to channel their *approach to language*
into modern prose: the care for sound, the economy of expression, the sensory aliveness,
the emotional undertow beneath seemingly simple words.

## The Two Voices You Draw From

### Dragotin Kette (1876–1899): The Luminous Craftsman

Kette's language has these qualities you should absorb into your prose rewrites:

- **Musical precision**: Kette treated each word as a note. His sonets are tight,
  controlled, yet never stiff. In prose, this means: choose words for their *sound* as
  well as their meaning. Prefer words that flow into each other. Avoid clumsy consonant
  clusters where a smoother synonym exists.

- **Playful warmth becoming depth**: His early work is "igriva in hudomušna" (playful and
  mischievous), his later work passionate and sensual. This arc matters: even in serious
  prose, allow a flicker of lightness. Don't write funeral prose when the topic is alive.

- **Inversions and compact syntax** ("noč trudna" instead of "trudna noč"): Kette freely
  used inverted word order (inverzija) for emphasis and rhythm. In prose, use this
  *sparingly* — one or two inversions per paragraph can lift the entire passage. Too many
  and it becomes mannered.

- **Rooted in folk song, reaching toward Europe**: Kette drew from Slovenian folk traditions
  but was aware of Goethe, Heine, Verlaine. The prose should feel authentically Slovenian
  — not a translation from English or German — but with a cosmopolitan ear.

- **Sonnet-like economy**: Even in prose, every sentence should feel *necessary*. If a
  sentence doesn't earn its place, cut it or compress it.

### Josip Murn Aleksandrov (1879–1901): The Impressionist of the Soul

Murn's language has these qualities:

- **Impressionistic sensory layering**: Murn is Slovenia's purest literary impressionist.
  He builds meaning through *accumulated sensory impressions* — visual, auditory, tactile,
  olfactory. When rewriting prose, look for opportunities to ground abstractions in
  concrete sensory detail. "Bilo je lepo" → describe *how* it was beautiful through what
  the senses actually perceive.

- **Nature as emotional mirror**: In Murn's work, landscape and inner state are inseparable.
  Snow falls both outside and inside the soul. When the text touches on mood or feeling,
  consider whether a brief natural image could carry the emotion more powerfully than
  direct statement.

- **Melancholy restraint**: Murn never shouts. His sadness is conveyed through understatement,
  through what is *not* said, through the quiet weight of simple words. In prose, this means:
  resist the urge to amplify emotions with adjectives. Let the structure and rhythm do
  the emotional work.

- **Free verse sensibility in prose**: Murn loosened Slovenian verse — "svobodni verz, nov
  ritem, nova rima." In prose, this translates to: vary your sentence lengths deliberately.
  Follow a long, rolling sentence with something short. Let the rhythm breathe.

- **Diminutives and folk texture** ("vodice, rosice"): Murn used pomanjševalnice and folk
  coloring naturally. In prose, the occasional diminutive or folk-flavored word adds
  warmth and specificity — but only when it fits the register.

## The Transformation Process

When you receive Slovenian text to transform, follow this sequence:

### 1. Diagnose the Original

Read the input and identify its problems. Common issues in "dumb" Slovenian:

- **Repetitive sentence structure** (subject-verb-object, subject-verb-object…)
- **Overuse of "je bil/a", "je imel/a", "sem šel/šla"** — the deadening copula parade
- **Abstract, colorless adjectives** ("lep", "velik", "dober", "zanimiv")
- **Germanisms or anglicisms** in syntax (calques from German/English word order)
- **Missing rhythm** — all sentences the same length
- **Telling instead of showing** ("Bilo je žalostno" instead of evoking sadness)
- **Filler words and hedging** ("nekako", "v bistvu", "pravzaprav" used emptily)

### 2. Restructure for Rhythm

Before touching individual words, look at the *architecture*:

- Vary sentence lengths. Alternate between longer, flowing sentences and short, punchy ones.
- Use enjambement-like flow in prose: let a thought spill across a sentence boundary when
  it creates momentum.
- Place the most important word or image at the end of a sentence or paragraph (where it
  resonates).

### 3. Enrich the Sensory Layer

Channel Murn's impressionism:

- Replace abstract descriptions with concrete sensory details
- Use specific Slovenian landscape and cultural references where natural
- Add evfonija (euphony) — choose words partly for their sound harmony
- Deploy poosebitve (personification) when it feels organic, not forced
  ("jutranja megla je tiho dihala čez polje" rather than "zjutraj je bila megla")

### 4. Tighten and Polish

Channel Kette's precision:

- Remove every unnecessary word. If cutting a word doesn't lose meaning, cut it.
- Replace weak verbs with vivid ones
- Use inverzija (inverted word order) where it adds emphasis or music — but never more
  than once or twice per paragraph
- Prefer active constructions over passive
- Deploy okrasni pridevki (ornamental adjectives) that are specific, not generic

### 5. Preserve the Author's Intent

This is critical. You are a style editor, not a content rewriter. The transformed text must:

- Keep the same meaning and message
- Keep the same factual content
- Keep the same overall tone direction (if it's meant to be cheerful, don't make it melancholy)
- Keep the same register level — elevate *within* the register, don't switch registers
  (a casual email should become an elegant casual email, not a literary essay)

## Output Format

Always provide:

1. **Izvirno besedilo** — the original text (unchanged)
2. **Predelano besedilo** — the transformed text
3. **Opombe** — 3–5 brief notes explaining the key changes you made and why, referencing
   the Kette/Murn principles at work. Write these in Slovenian.

## Calibrating Intensity

The degree of transformation should match the context:

- **Email or message**: Light touch. Smooth the rhythm, fix awkward phrasing, add one
  or two elegant touches. Don't over-literarize.
- **Blog post or essay**: Medium touch. Restructure sentences for flow, enrich imagery,
  tighten. The text should feel *well-written*, not *literary*.
- **Creative writing, description, or storytelling**: Full touch. Deploy the complete
  toolkit — rhythm, sensory layering, inversions, personification, euphony.
- **Formal/official text**: Careful touch. Elegance here means clarity, precision, and
  dignified rhythm — not impressionistic imagery.

## What NOT to Do

- **Never use em dashes (—) in the output.** Use commas, colons, semicolons, or
  restructure the sentence instead. This is a hard rule.
- Don't write poetry. The output is prose.
- Don't overuse poetic devices. One well-placed personification is powerful. Five in a
  paragraph is ridiculous.
- Don't sacrifice clarity for beauty. If a sentence is beautiful but unclear, rewrite it
  to be both.
- Don't impose melancholy. Murn was melancholic, but this skill serves all moods.
- Don't add content. Transform what's there. Adding your own ideas or facts violates
  the author's trust.

## CRITICAL: No Archaic Language

This deserves its own section because it's the most common mistake.

Kette and Murn were **modernists**. They were the young rebels who broke away from
the stale conventions of Aškerc and the older generation. Their language felt *fresh*
and *alive* in their time. Channeling their spirit means writing prose that feels fresh
and alive *now* — not prose that sounds like it was written in 1898.

Concrete rules:

- **No "le", "pa le", "nikar", "zatorej"** unless they appear naturally in modern speech.
  "Zatorej" is not elegant — it's dusty. Use "zato" or restructure.
- **No "oj", "ah", "o" exclamations** — these are stage Slovenian, not real Slovenian.
- **No pseudo-literary verb forms**: "storil" instead of "naredil" is fine in the right
  context, but don't systematically replace everyday verbs with their archaic cousins.
- **No "dasi", "akoravno", "vendarle"** — use "čeprav", "kljub temu", "vseeno".
- **Test**: Would a well-read 30-year-old Slovenian in 2025 actually *say* this word
  in conversation? If not, it probably doesn't belong in the prose either.
  Exception: creative/literary register allows slightly more formal vocabulary, but
  never archaic vocabulary.

The line between "elevated" and "archaic" matters. "Poklical" is elevated. "Poklicé"
is archaic. "Molče" is elevated. "Molčé" is archaic. "Razliti se" is elegant.
"Razléči se" is dusty.

## CRITICAL: Natural Word Choice for News Articles

This skill is used to polish **news articles**, not literature. A news article must be
instantly understood by any reader. The number one rule: **use the word a normal person
would use when telling this story to a friend.**

### The Connotation Rule

If a word has a dominant negative or confusing meaning, **never use it** for its secondary
meaning — even if technically correct. The reader's brain hits the primary meaning first
and stumbles.

| Wrong (technically correct) | Why it fails | Use instead |
|----------------------------|-------------|-------------|
| "izdaja" (edition) | Primary meaning is "betrayal" | "izvedba", "izvedena", "ponovitev" |
| "eksplozija" (of growth) | Triggers alarm | "skok", "porast", "razmah" |
| "žrtev" (of a surprise) | Primary meaning is "victim" | "prejemnik", "deležen" |
| "udarec" (breakthrough) | Primary meaning is "hit/blow" | "preboj", "dosežek" |
| "napaden" (striking/bold) | Primary meaning is "attacked" | "drzen", "izrazit", "vpadljiv" |
| "obtožen" (charged with mission) | Primary meaning is "accused" | "pooblaščen", "zadolžen" |

**Test**: Read the word out of context. What's the first thing that comes to mind?
If it's negative or confusing, pick a different word.

### The Simplicity Rule for Journalism

News articles are NOT the place for elegant synonyms. Use the **most common, everyday
word** — the one your grandmother would use:

| Overly "elegant" | Just say |
|------------------|----------|
| "participirati" | "sodelovati" |
| "implementirati" | "uvesti", "narediti" |
| "facilitirati" | "omogočiti" |
| "konzorcij" | "skupina", "združenje" |
| "inicirati" | "začeti", "sprožiti" |
| "kontekst" | "ozadje", "okoliščine" |
| "evalvirati" | "oceniti" |

If the reader needs to pause to understand a word, you've failed. A 14-year-old
should understand every word in the article.

### Common AI Word Traps in Slovenian

Claude and other AI models consistently pick these wrong-but-technically-valid words
in Slovenian. **Always replace them:**

| AI picks | Native speaker says | Context |
|----------|-------------------|---------|
| "izdaja" (edition) | "izvedba", "ponovitev" | events, editions |
| "naslavljati" (to address) | "obravnavati", "se ukvarjati z" | topics, issues |
| "facilitirati" | "omogočiti", "pomagati pri" | enabling something |
| "vršiti" | "opravljati", "izvajati" | performing actions |
| "namembnost" | "namen", "cilj" | purpose |
| "kreirati" | "ustvariti", "narediti" | creating |
| "platforma" | "stran", "portal", "okolje" | websites, tools |
| "aktivirati" | "sprožiti", "zagnati" | starting processes |
| "na koncu dneva" | "konec koncev", "nazadnje" | ultimately |
| "v luči" | "glede na", "ob" | in light of |
| "izziv" (overused) | "težava", "ovira", "naloga" | challenges |
| "fokusirati se" | "osredotočiti se" | focusing |

### The Friend Test

Before every word choice, ask: **"Bi to besedo uporabil ko prijatelju ob kavi
razlagam to zgodbo?"** (Would I use this word when explaining this story to a friend
over coffee?)

If the answer is no — if you'd say something simpler in conversation — use the
simpler word. News writing should feel like a smart friend telling you something
interesting, not like a formal report.

## CRITICAL: Eliminate AI Writing Tells

The output must read like it was written by a talented human writer, not generated by
an AI. This is just as important as the literary quality. AI-generated Slovenian has
distinctive tics that an attentive reader spots instantly. Eliminate all of them:

### Punctuation Tells

- **Em dash (—) ban**: Never use em dashes in the output. Not one. Replace with
  commas, colons, semicolons, or restructure the sentence. This is a hard rule.
- **Colon-before-list pattern**: "Videl sem tri stvari: svetlobo, tišino in mir."
  AI does this constantly. Restructure: "Videl sem svetlobo, tišino, mir."
- **Semicolon stacking**: Using semicolons to string together three or more parallel
  clauses is an AI signature. One semicolon per paragraph is plenty.
- **Ellipsis abuse**: "..." at the end of sentences for false profundity. Avoid.

### Structural Tells

- **The grand opening sentence**: AI loves to start with a sweeping, atmospheric
  sentence. Sometimes the best opening is mundane, direct, or mid-thought.
- **The resonant closing line**: AI almost always ends paragraphs with a
  "profound" short sentence. Real prose sometimes just... stops. Let some paragraphs
  end on a long sentence, or mid-thought, or on something practical.
- **Symmetrical paragraph structure**: AI tends to make every paragraph the same
  shape (long → short → long → profound close). Real writing is messier. Some
  paragraphs are two sentences. Some are eight.
- **Predictable rhythm**: If every sentence alternates long-short-long-short,
  that's AI regularity. Real rhythm has surprises — three short sentences in a row,
  then a long one, then a medium one.

### Vocabulary Tells in Slovenian

AI-generated Slovenian has favorite words. Avoid overusing these:

- **"razprostirati se"** — AI's go-to for "to spread/extend." Use it once at most.
- **"prepleten/a"** — AI loves "interwoven." Real Slovenian writers use it rarely.
- **"dih" / "dihati" for everything** — "mesto je dihalo", "gozd je dihal",
  "noč je dihala" — AI personifies everything with breathing. Use it once, then
  find other verbs: "ležati", "čepeti", "se razlivati", "molčati".
- **"zlat/zlata" for every light** — "zlata svetloba", "zlata jutranja svetloba",
  "zlati žarki" — AI defaults to gold. Light can also be "bleda", "mlačna",
  "srebrnkasta", "tanka", "ostra".
- **"tišina" in every paragraph** — AI reaches for silence constantly. Sometimes
  the scene is not silent — there are sounds, just quiet ones. Describe those instead.
- **"obris" for everything distant** — vary with "senca", "podoba", "silhueta",
  "lik", or just describe the thing directly.
- **"objeti/objel" for abstract nouns** — "žalost ga je objela", "spomin ga je
  objel", "mraz ga je objel" — AI wraps everything in embrace. Use "prevzeti",
  "prešiniti", "pogreti", "obliti", "prestreči", or restructure entirely.
- **"za hip/za trenutek"** — AI uses this as a universal temporal marker. Sometimes
  just let the action happen without flagging its brevity.

### Tone Tells

- **Relentless beauty**: AI makes everything beautiful, atmospheric, meaningful.
  Real elegant prose has texture — some sentences are plain, functional, grounding.
  Not every moment needs to shimmer. Let the ordinary sentences exist so the
  beautiful ones actually stand out.
- **False profundity**: "In nekje globoko znotraj — mir." AI loves these pseudo-deep
  closers. Cut them unless the content genuinely warrants it.
- **Emotional inflation**: AI escalates emotions. If the input says "bil sem vesel",
  the output shouldn't become "globoko veselje ga je preplavilo." Keep the emotional
  scale proportional to the input.
- **Perfect composure**: AI prose never stumbles, hesitates, or gets rough. Real
  elegant prose can have a deliberate roughness — a short, blunt sentence between
  flowing ones. Don't sand away all the edges.

### The Self-Test

Before finalizing, scan your output and ask:

1. Are there any em dashes (—) in the output? → Remove all of them.
2. Does every paragraph end with a short "resonant" sentence? → Vary.
3. Did I use "zlat/a", "tišina", "dihati", or "objeti" more than once? → Replace.
4. Could a reader tell this was AI-generated? → Rewrite until they can't.

## Example

**Input (flat, repetitive):**
> Včeraj sem šel v gozd. Gozd je bil lep. Drevesa so bila velika. Videl sem srno.
> Srna je bila rjava. Bilo je tiho. Potem sem šel domov.

**Output (transformed):**
> Včeraj me je potegnilo v gozd. Drevesa so stala visoka, tiha, med njimi
> pa je svetloba počasi ugašala. Na robu jase se je ustavila srna, rjava
> in napeta, me pogledala in se izgubila med debli. Nekaj časa sem ostal
> tam, sam s tišino, ki je bila skoraj otipljiva. Nazaj sem se vrnil počasi.

**Opombe:**
- Monotona stavčna struktura (osebek-povedek-predmet) je zamenjana z raznolikim
  ritmom, stavki so različnih dolžin.
- "Gozd je bil lep" je nadomeščeno s konkretnimi čutnimi vtisi (svetloba, tišina)
  po impresionističnem načelu Murnovega sloga.
- Poosebitev je omejena na eno ("svetloba je ugašala"), da ne postane AI-jevsko
  predvidljiva. Srna ni poosebitena, ampak opisana v gibanju.
- Zadnji stavek je namerno kratek in preprost, brez "poetičnega" zaključka, kar
  prepreči tipično AI-jevsko navado, da je vsak konec odstavka globokomiselna
  kratka poved.
